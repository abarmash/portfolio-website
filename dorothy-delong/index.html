Dorothy De Long (Concept Client) | Single-Page Portfolio Website Design

Tools Used: Adobe XD

Project Overview: Dorothy De Long is a photographer who was looking for a low-maintenance single-page portfolio site to showcase her work. Until now, Dorothy has been relying on posting her work on Facebook and mailing a physical portfolio to potential clients, like galleries and museums. Dorothy was looking for a clean website that featured an artist’s statement, headshot, contact information, and of course her photography portfolio.

Project Deliverables: Single-Page Portfolio Site Design Comp

Project Goals: There were two project goals for Dorothy’s single-page website:
1. It should entice magazines to purchase her photos for publication. Up until now, she hasn’t had a good place to showcase her work when magazines ask for a portfolio.
2. It should also help get her work shown in museums, both locally and abroad. Since Dorothy has had to mail a physical portfolio to museums, an online portfolio site would allow her work to be shown much more widely.

Design Process: After learning about Dorothy’s needs and setting the project goals, I started by developing the site structure and drawing a paper wireframe. Then, I built a digital wireframe, chose the typography and the color scheme (using some of Dorothy’s photos), and built a hi-fi design composition. After receiving some feedback from Dorothy, I made a few revisions and handed off the final design files.
